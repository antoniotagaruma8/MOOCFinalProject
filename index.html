Improving Oral Fluency through Debating and Games

MOOC Final Project Pedagogical Strategy

Interactive Game-Based Learning

The Challenge: Moving Beyond Rote Memorization

In many language learning environments, students become proficient at prepared speeches and rote memorization, but struggle with spontaneous, authentic conversation. The challenge is to bridge the gap between passive knowledge and active fluency. This project addresses that challenge by implementing dynamic, interactive activities that simulate real-world communication scenarios.

The pedagogical framework is inspired by debate and public speaking models like Model United Nations (MUN), where participants must think on their feet, articulate arguments clearly, and respond to unexpected points. By adapting these principles for the language classroom, we can create an environment that fosters not just linguistic accuracy, but also conversational agility and confidence.

This approach draws heavily from the methodologies of the Global Classroom program, which specializes in such interactive learning experiences. The activities in this case study, from structured rebuttals to creative impersonations, are designed to systematically build the skills required for effective, unprepared oral discourse. The goal is to equip students with the tools to handle the pressure of real-time conversation and develop true communicative competence.

A Toolkit of Speaking Games

This case study focuses on four dynamic games, each designed to target a specific set of conversational skills in a fun and engaging way.

  • If I Ruled the World: A creative icebreaker that doubles as a memory challenge, perfect for practicing conditional clauses and building rapport.
  • I Couldn’t Disagree More: A fast-paced game of rebuttal that trains students to build arguments and justify their opinions under pressure.
  • Point of Information Game: A formal debate simulation that teaches students how to gracefully handle interruptions and think on their feet.
  • Balloon Debates: A role-playing game that encourages persuasive storytelling and creative argumentation.

Activity Objectives

The primary goals were to improve oral fluency and confidence in speaking English. Key objectives included developing quick thinking, strengthening listening and memory skills, practicing the expression and justification of opinions, improving argumentation, encouraging collaboration among students, and fostering creativity in communication.

Development

Preparation Before Execution

Thorough preparation was key. This involved reviewing and adapting game rules for clarity, selecting engaging topics, and preparing role cards. The classroom was arranged to facilitate interaction, and necessary materials like timers and prompts were prepared in coordination with the subject teacher.

Activity Development

All four activities were successfully carried out during English and History Class with teacher support. The execution involved various formats, including students standing in a circle for introductions, working in pairs for debates, teacher modeling for demonstrating techniques, and class-wide voting for balloon debates.

Support

Tools & Materials

A variety of materials were used, including Global Classroom activities, custom printed role cards and statements, a timer/stopwatch for debates, a whiteboard/projector for instructions, and audio/video recordings for feedback.

Classroom Integration

The project was integrated into the curriculum by coordinating with the subject teacher and aligning the activities with the speaking units. This approach also supported the development of key competencies outlined in the LOMLOE framework.

Attention to Diversity

To ensure all students could participate effectively, various support mechanisms were in place. These included providing sentence starters and vocabulary lists, allowing stronger speakers to model responses, options for shorter contributions for shy students, and strategic grouping to balance skill levels.

Assessment

Evaluation Criteria

Students were assessed on the clarity/coherence of their English; ability to express/justify; listening skills; participation/engagement; ability to respond to rebuttals; respectful communication/teamwork.

Assessment and Improvement Proposal

Activities worked well, created energetic/supportive environment, students were highly engaged. Suggested Improvement: Future iterations could include a structured rubric for peer-assessment.
